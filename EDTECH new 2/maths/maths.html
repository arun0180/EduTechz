Addition

Addition is one of the basic arithmetic operations used to combine two or more numbers to find their total or sum. It is often represented by the plus sign (+).

Key points about addition:

Operands: The numbers that are being added are called "addends."

Result: The result of addition is called the "sum."

Example:

If you add 3 and 5, you write it as:
3+5=8
Here, 3 and 5 are the addends, and 8 is the sum.

Multiplication

Multiplication is one of the basic operations in arithmetic and mathematics. It is essentially repeated addition. When you multiply two numbers, you are adding one number to itself repeatedly a certain number of times.

Key Concept: If you multiply two numbers, for example, 4 and 3, it means you are adding the number 4 three times. So, 4 √ó 3 is the same as: 4+4+4=12

Multiplication Terminology:

Multiplicand: The number being multiplied (in the example, 4).

Multiplier: The number by which the multiplicand is multiplied (in the example, 3).

Product: The result of the multiplication (in the example, 12). Example: Let's take a simple example: 5 √ó 2

Division

Division is one of the basic operations in arithmetic, and it is essentially the process of splitting or sharing something into equal parts. In division, you take a number (the dividend) and divide it by another number (the divisor) to find out how many times the divisor fits into the dividend. The result is called the quotient.

Key Terminology:

Dividend: The number you are dividing (the total amount you want to split).

Divisor: The number by which you are dividing the dividend (the number of parts or groups).

Quotient: The result of the division (how many times the divisor fits into the dividend).

Remainder: The part left over after division, when the dividend is not perfectly divisible by the divisor.

Basic Division: For example, if you have 12 √∑ 3, you're asking, "How many times does 3 fit into 12?" Dividend: 12 Divisor: 3 Quotient: 4 (because 3 fits into 12 exactly 4 times). So,
12√∑3=4
